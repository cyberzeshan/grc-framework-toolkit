# GRC Program Maturity Model

> **Purpose:** Assess the maturity of an organization's Governance, Risk, and Compliance program across five dimensions and produce a prioritized improvement roadmap
> **Audience:** CISOs, GRC program managers, security directors, executive sponsors
> **Use:** Conduct this assessment annually and after major organizational changes; use output to prioritize GRC investments and communicate program status to leadership

---

## Overview

This maturity model evaluates GRC program effectiveness across **five dimensions**, each rated on a **1–5 scale**. Unlike framework-specific tier assessments (e.g., NIST CSF tiers), this model assesses the *program management* infrastructure around security — the processes, people, and organizational integration that make controls sustainable.

**The five dimensions:**

| # | Dimension | What It Measures |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| 1 | **Governance** | Executive ownership, accountability structures, and policy infrastructure |
| 2 | **Risk Management** | Systematic identification, assessment, treatment, and monitoring of risk |
| 3 | **Compliance** | Regulatory tracking, audit readiness, and evidence management |
| 4 | **Control Operations** | Consistency and sustainability of security control implementation |
| 5 | **Metrics and Reporting** | Measurement of program effectiveness and communication to stakeholders |

---

## Maturity Scale

| Level | Label | Description |
|:---:|:---|:---|
| **1** | Initial | Activities are ad hoc, undocumented, and reactive. Success depends on heroic individual effort. |
| **2** | Developing | Key processes are defined but inconsistently applied. Some documentation exists. |
| **3** | Defined | Processes are documented, approved, and consistently followed across the organization. |
| **4** | Managed | Processes are measured. Data drives decisions. Deviations are detected and corrected. |
| **5** | Optimizing | Continuous improvement is embedded. The program adapts proactively to new threats and business changes. |

## Maturity Level Profiles

### Level 1 — Initial (Score ≤ 40%)
**Situation:** Security is reactive. Incidents drive action. No consistent processes. Individual heroes keep things running.

**Risk:** Very High. The organization is highly vulnerable to commodity threats. A single incident could cause catastrophic damage.

**Immediate priorities:**
- Appoint a named security owner with executive authority
- Complete an asset inventory and initial risk assessment
- Implement MFA on all internet-facing systems
- Draft and get executive sign-off on a basic security policy
- Establish an incident response procedure

**Target timeline to Level 2:** 6–12 months with focused effort

---

### Level 2 — Developing (Score 41–58%)
**Situation:** Key processes exist on paper but aren't consistently followed. Some good controls are in place, but coverage is incomplete and auditable evidence is sparse.

**Risk:** High. Good-faith effort is visible but gaps remain that threat actors can exploit.

**Priorities:**
- Operationalize the risk assessment process — move from ad hoc to scheduled
- Achieve consistent patch compliance across all systems
- Implement security awareness training with tracked completion
- Establish a vendor risk management baseline
- Build an evidence library for customer security questionnaires

**Target timeline to Level 3:** 12–18 months

---

### Level 3 — Defined (Score 59–74%)
**Situation:** Processes are documented, approved, and followed consistently. The organization can demonstrate security practices to auditors. Controls work most of the time.

**Risk:** Moderate. The organization is defensible against commodity attacks. Targeted attacks or insider threats remain a concern.

**Priorities:**
- Pursue external certification (ISO 27001, SOC 2 Type II)
- Implement continuous monitoring and automated alerting
- Mature the vendor risk program beyond questionnaires (continuous monitoring, contract provisions)
- Integrate security into SDLC and DevOps pipelines
- Establish a formal metrics program with board-level reporting

**Target timeline to Level 4:** 12–24 months

---

### Level 4 — Managed (Score 75–88%)
**Situation:** The program is data-driven. KPIs are tracked, reported, and used to drive investment decisions. Deviations are detected and corrected quickly. Leadership has good visibility.

**Risk:** Low-Moderate. The organization is well-defended against most threats. Advanced persistent threats (APTs) remain a challenge.

**Priorities:**
- Integrate threat intelligence into real-time risk decisions
- Implement red team or adversary simulation exercises
- Achieve continuous compliance monitoring (automated evidence collection)
- Stand up or enhance a Security Operations Center (SOC) or MDR capability
- Build a formalized GRC tool or platform to replace spreadsheets

**Target timeline to Level 5:** 24–36 months

---

### Level 5 — Optimizing (Score 89–100%)
**Situation:** The GRC program continuously improves based on lessons learned, threat intelligence, and benchmarking. Security is a business enabler. The organization leads its sector peers in security maturity.

**Risk:** Managed. Residual risk is understood and accepted. The organization can detect and respond to sophisticated threats.

**Priorities:**
- Share threat intelligence with sector ISAC and peers
- Publish transparency reports or security commitments
- Contribute to open-source security frameworks and benchmarks
- Continuously challenge assumptions — run red team operations, test incident response with live exercises
- Mentor and elevate sector security standards
